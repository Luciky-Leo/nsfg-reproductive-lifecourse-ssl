# Figure Alt Text For JAMIA Open

## Figure 1

Workflow diagram showing the use of National Survey of Family Growth female respondent and pregnancy files, cycle harmonization from 2011-2013 through 2022-2023, construction of a respondent-level life-course matrix, masked tabular self-supervised encoding, phenotype discovery, temporal survey validation, and robustness checks.

## Figure 2

Multi-panel figure summarizing cohort linkage and analytic matrix construction. Panels show respondent counts and pregnancy-record history coverage by survey cycle, primary input-domain counts, skip-pattern missingness by cycle, feature-selection flow, and endpoint prevalence across cycles with change from 2011-2013 to 2022-2023.

## Figure 3

Multi-panel phenotype-discovery figure. Panels show principal-component visualization of SSL embeddings with phenotype ellipses, phenotype sizes, leading profile drivers, and k-selection diagnostics including a metrics heatmap, silhouette width, and bootstrap adjusted Rand index.

## Figure 4

Annotated phenotype profile heatmap for the 2022-2023 cycle. Rows show selected reproductive life-course descriptors and endpoint-related characteristics, columns show phenotypes P0, P1, and P2, colors show standardized profile deviations, and cell labels show weighted raw means or prevalences.

## Figure 5

Endpoint-enrichment and model-summary figure. Panel A shows survey-weighted phenotype prevalence ratios with bootstrap confidence intervals for five reproductive-health endpoints. Panel B shows AUPRC enrichment over baseline prevalence for phenotype-only, SSL-embedding, and SSL-plus-phenotype summaries.
